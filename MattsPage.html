USE OF EXOSUITS IN MEDICINE

Exosuits (powered exo-skeletons) are wearable frame powered by various technologies in order to improve the users ability to carry out tasks. They originally started out as a project by the U.S. military but are now having a big impact within the medical field, sepcifically in helping to rehabilitate people with mobility issues.

Notable examples of this include:

  • Cyberyne's HAL exo-skeleton helping patients in the US deal with lower limb disorders
  • Clinatec and the University of Grenoble collaborating to trial a mind-controlled exoskeleton to help patients suffering from paralysis
  • ReWalk Robotics' ReStore soft exoskeleton being used to treat patients recovering from strokes

The implementation of these technologies in the medical field show a lot of promise in their potential to change the way we treat patients with mobility issues and stand as a testement to the positive impacts that computer science and robotics can have on our society.
